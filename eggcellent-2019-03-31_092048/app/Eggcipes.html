Soft Boiled

1. Fill a pot with water and bring it to the boil.
2. Place eggs in and allow to boil for 5-6 minutes.
3. Taking a knife, tap the shell until it breaks and then slice the top off before eating.

Hard Boiled

1. Fill a pot with water and bring it to the boil.
2. Place eggs in and allow to boil for 10-12 minutes.
3. After removing the eggs from the pot, to peel the shell away run the eggs under cold water then gently tap and roll them on a counter.

Scrambled

1. Crack two eggs into a small bowl, season with salt and pepper, and beat with a fork until blended into a uniform colour.
2. Heat oil or butter in a pan. When hot, tip in the egg mixture.
3. Allow it to cook, taking care to flip the mixture so there are no wet patches: everything should be cooked through.

Omelette

1. Crack two eggs into a small bowl, season with salt and pepper, and beat with a fork until blended into a uniform colour.
2. Melt butter in a pan before adding in the eggs.
3. When it begins to cook but is still runny, add in any other ingredients, such as cheese, spinach, or mushrooms.
4. Using a spatula, flip over the omeette to ensure it is thoroughly cooked.

Fried

1. Heat butter in a pan over a medium-high heat.
2. Crack eggs and slip into the pan, one at a time, taking care not to get any shell in. Reduce the heat to low.
3. Cook until the white is completely set and the yolk is still runny; this should be 5-6 minutes.
4. Using a spatula, flip over the eggs and cook the other side for a further 5-6 minutes, before seasoning and serving.

Poached

1. Heat water in a pot and add vinegar.
2. Crack the egg into the pot, gently swirling the pot to create a "whirpool" of water. This pulls the white of the egg together.
3. Leave it in the water to cook for about 5 minutes then gently remove.
